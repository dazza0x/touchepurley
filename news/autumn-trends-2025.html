Autumn Trends 2025

The cuts, colours and styles that are defining this season — and how to get the look.

11 October 2025 2 min read Trends

Autumn is arguably the most exciting season for hair. The shift in mood — deeper tones, richer textures, a new energy — creates the perfect opportunity for a change. This year, the trends feel both fresh and grounded, combining editorial-inspired shapes with effortless wearability. Here's what's turning heads this autumn.

Bobs Are Back — With a Twist

The bob has never really left, but in autumn 2025 it's having a full renaissance. Forget stiff, helmet-like precision — this season's bobs are soft, lived-in and full of personality.

  • The Riviera Bob — A relaxed, slightly undone version with gentle texture and soft ends. Think French coastal ease rather than sharp geometry.
  • The Jawline Bob — Cut to sit right at the jaw, this length is universally flattering and works beautifully with face-framing highlights or balayage.
  • The Blunt Bob — For those who want impact. A clean, precise edge that photographs beautifully and exudes confidence.
  • The Textured Bob — Soft movement created by point-cutting or texturising shears for a low-maintenance, high-style result.

Layers and Movement

Long, flat hair is firmly out. Autumn 2025 is all about movement — styles that flow, bounce and react to the way you move.

  • The Butterfly Cut — A layered style with short, wispy layers framing the face and longer, flowing lengths below. Romantic and dramatic in equal measure.
  • The Modern Shag — Updated for 2025, this cut features heavily textured layers, curtain bangs and plenty of rock-and-roll energy. Suits almost every hair type.
  • The Long Wolf Cut — Edgy, layered and surprisingly versatile. Pairs beautifully with rich, deep colour for an autumn-appropriate look.

Bangs — From Wispy to Bold

Bangs are everywhere this season, with options to suit every face shape and comfort level.

  • Curtain Bangs — Soft, parted in the middle and feathered to the sides. The most face-flattering bang option, and incredibly easy to grow out if you change your mind.
  • Birkin Bangs — Inspired by Jane Birkin's signature fringe. Wispy, effortless and undone. The anti-precision bang.
  • Blunt Bangs — A strong statement. Works best with structured cuts like the blunt bob or a sleek lob.
  • Wispy Micro Bangs — For the truly adventurous. Short, airy and very editorial.

Short Cuts & Pixies

Short hair is making a confident comeback. The bixie (a hybrid between a bob and a pixie) is having a major moment, offering a fresh, low-maintenance cut that still allows for styling versatility. The curly pixie — tailored specifically for natural texture — is one of the most celebrated cuts of the season.

Creative Braids & Half-Up Styles

For longer lengths, braided elements and half-up styles offer a way to look polished while keeping things interesting. Crown braids, twisted half-ups and loose boho plaits all feature heavily in autumn 2025 editorial looks.

Colour Trends: Rich, Warm Tones

Colour this autumn follows the season's palette. Chestnuts, warm caramels, toasted ambers and deep auburns dominate. Balayage continues to reign, but with richer, more saturated base tones rather than the bleached-out roots of previous years. Brunettes are adding dimension with copper and reddish notes. Blondes are warming up — goodbye icy platinum, hello golden wheat.

Why These Styles Work for Our Purley Clients

The common thread running through all of this season's key trends is effortlessness. These aren't styles that demand daily effort — they're cuts and colours designed to look good with minimal styling and grow out gracefully.

That's especially important for our clients who want to look great without spending an hour in front of the mirror every morning. Our stylists will always tailor any trend to your specific hair type, lifestyle and face shape — because the best hairstyle isn't necessarily the one that's most fashionable, but the one that's most you.
